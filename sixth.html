SOME SUCCESS STORIES

REGINA AGYARE

Regina, who describes herself as a "coder who loves a challenge," didn't even know any female IT role models when she was a child. She enjoys working with girls now and educating them about the opportunities in math, science, and technology. We appreciate Regina sharing more of her story, her secrets to success, and her future journey with women who want to work in technology or entrepreneurship. Her passion is using technology to spark social change, and she does this through her company, Soronko Solutions. She is currently working on a location-based mobile application for Accra, Ghanaian SMEs, to help them become more visible and competitive with larger companies.

MARK ATTA MENSAH

Mark Atta Mensah teaches computer science at the University of Ghana, located in the nation's capital, Accra. That same university awarded him a Master of Philosophy in Computer Science. He used to teach at the business, technology, and fashion school BlueCrest University College. Mensah uses the Oracle Academy curriculum to teach computer skills. In 2015, he received training in database design and SQL programming, as well as Java programming, while working as a graduate teaching assistant. Prior to entering the teaching profession, he later used those skills in industrial jobs.

ETHEL COFIE

A highly sought-after IT specialist with over 15 years of experience, Ethel Cofie is the founder and CEO of EDEL Technology Consulting. She has held various positions in the UK, Nigeria, and Sierra Leone in addition to serving as Vodafone's head of commercial solutions. In addition to serving on the boards of several African companies, she established Women in Tech, Africa, a platform that brings together women in technology from more than 30 nations. She has continued to be successful in the tech industry, made a greater impact, and is a strong advocate for encouraging more women to work in tech.

MICHEAL AGBO TETTEY SOLI

A solutions engineer with over ten years of expertise in software engineering and algorithm design, as well as a critical thinker. Solving complicated problems with mathematics is just another chance to highlight the elegance and beauty of the subject when one has a solid mathematical foundation. Developed and oversaw a group of software engineers to create, implement, and use custom software solutions to fulfill the demands of customers in various industries. Driving the company's technical vision and ensuring the development of high-quality software products in terms of effectiveness, maintainability, and performance are my responsibilities.

FARIDA BEDWEI

Farida was born in Lagos, Nigeria, and is a Ghanaian. Despite having been diagnosed with cerebral palsy, she is a living example of how nothing can stop her from pursuing her goals in life! She has a B.Sc. in computer science and a diploma in e-technology. She has over 15 years of solid experience as a software developer, software engineer, and computer scientist, and she is well-known for her creative ideas and sharp technological mind.

MICHEAL AGBO TETTEY SOLI

Currently serving as the Head of the University of Ghana's Department of Computer Science is Jamal-Deen Abdulai. He is a computer science professor with more than ten years of professional experience in both academia and business. He received his MPhil in 2006 and his PhD in computer science in 2009 from the University of Glasgow in the United Kingdom. Before that, in 2002, he graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST). Before joining the University of Ghana, Dr. Abdulai was employed as a lecturer at the GIMPA School of Technology from 2009 to 2013 and as a senior research assistant at the University for Development Studies (UDS) from 2002 to 2004. He has instructed several computer science courses.
